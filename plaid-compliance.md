# Plaid Compliance Requirements by Category

## Development and Vulnerability Management

### Attest that your organization performs vulnerability scanning
**Description:** Actively performing vulnerability scans on employee and contractor machines, as well as production assets, is essential for detecting and addressing security vulnerabilities. It is highly recommended that your organization implement regular, automated vulnerability scans to identify potential security weaknesses in laptops, servers, and other critical systems. Promptly patching identified vulnerabilities will help mitigate the risk of security breaches, ensuring that your systems remain secure and resilient against emerging threats. Regular vulnerability scanning is a key component of a robust security program.

### Attest that your organization patches identified vulnerabilities within a defined SLA
**Description:** Promptly patching identified vulnerabilities will help mitigate the risk of security breaches, ensuring that your systems remain secure and resilient against emerging threats. Regular vulnerability scanning is a key component of a robust security program. It is recommended that your organization establish and adhere to a defined Service Level Agreement (SLA) for patching vulnerabilities. This will ensure that vulnerabilities are addressed in a timely manner, reducing the window of exposure and enhancing your overall security posture.

### Attest that your organization monitors end-of-life (EOL) software in use and updates policies to include EOL management practices
**Description:** Monitoring end-of-life (EOL) software is essential for maintaining the security and stability of your systems. EOL software no longer receives updates or patches, making it vulnerable to security risks. It is recommended that your organization implement policies to actively monitor and manage EOL software, ensuring timely updates or replacements.

## Governance and Risk Management

### Attest that your organization has created an Information Security Policy (ISP)
**Description:** The ISP serves as the foundation for your information security program, outlining the organization's commitment to protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets. Your organization should develop a formal, documented ISP that: 1) defines objectives, 2) establishes accountability, 3) establishes scope, 4) is approved by individuals with management responsibilities, and 5) is kept up to date.

## Identity and Access Management

### Attest that your organization has implemented role-based access control (RBAC)
**Description:** Implementing role-based access control (RBAC) is crucial for managing user permissions effectively and minimizing the risk of unauthorized access. RBAC assigns access rights based on roles within the organization, ensuring that individuals only have access to the information necessary for their job functions. It is recommended that your organization develop and enforce an RBAC system to streamline access management and enhance security.

### Attest that your organization has implemented multi-factor authentication (MFA) on internal systems that store or process consumer data
**Description:** To enhance the security of your corporate environment, it is strongly recommended to implement multi-factor authentication (MFA) on all internal systems, particularly those used to manage and interact with sensitive data. MFA strengthens your security by requiring employees to verify their identity through two or more factors, significantly reducing the risk of unauthorized access due to compromised credentials. By enforcing MFA, you can better protect your organization's critical assets, prevent potential breaches, and improve overall security resilience.

### Attest that your organization has implemented multi-factor authentication (MFA) on the consumer-facing application where Plaid Link is deployed
**Description:** To enhance security for consumers using your mobile and/or web applications where Plaid Link is deployed, it is highly recommended to implement multi-factor authentication (MFA). MFA adds an extra layer of protection by requiring users to provide two or more verification factors to gain access. This significantly reduces the risk of fraud and unauthorized access due to compromised credentials and helps safeguard sensitive financial data. Implementing MFA will strengthen your overall security posture and build greater trust with your consumers.

### Attest that your organization has implemented automated de-provisioning/modification of access for terminated or transferred employees
**Description:** Automating the de-provisioning and modification of access for terminated or transferred employees is essential for preventing unauthorized access to your organization's resources. It is recommended that your organization implement automated processes to immediately revoke or adjust access rights when employees leave or change roles. This helps ensure that access permissions are always current and minimizes the risk of insider threats. Integrating these automated processes with your HR systems can further streamline access management.

### Attest that your organization has implemented a zero trust access architecture
**Description:** Implementing a zero trust access architecture is critical for enhancing your organization's security posture by continuously verifying user and device identities. Zero trust principles ensure that no entity is trusted by default, and access is granted based on strict verification processes. It is recommended that your organization adopt a zero trust framework, including multi-factor authentication, continuous monitoring, and micro-segmentation.

### Attest that your organization has implemented centralized identity and access management solutions
**Description:** Centralized identity and access management solutions are vital for efficiently managing and securing user identities across your organization. These solutions provide a unified platform for authenticating users, managing access rights, and monitoring access activities. Centralized management enhances visibility, simplifies compliance, and reduces administrative overhead.

### Attest that your organization has implemented a defined and documented access control policy
**Description:** Establishing a defined and documented access control policy is essential for ensuring consistent and secure management of access to your organization's resources. It is recommended that your organization develop a comprehensive access control policy that outlines the principles of least privilege, role-based access controls, and procedures for granting, modifying, and revoking access. This policy should be regularly reviewed and updated to adapt to changing security requirements and ensure compliance with industry standards.

## Infrastructure and Network Security

### Attest that your organization has implemented data encryption at-rest controls
**Description:** It is recommended that your organization implement strong encryption methods, such as AES-256, to protect this data while it is stored. This practice helps reduce the risk of data breaches and unauthorized access, ensuring that consumer financial data remains secure even if storage systems are compromised.

### Attest that your organization secures data in-transit communications
**Description:** Encrypting data in transit between clients and servers is crucial for protecting sensitive information from interception and tampering. It is highly recommended that your organization implement Transport Layer Security (TLS) 1.2 or higher to ensure that data transmitted over the network is securely encrypted. This will help safeguard confidential information, maintain data integrity, and build trust with your clients by demonstrating a commitment to strong security practices.

## Privacy

### Attest that your organization has published a privacy policy
**Description:** Developing a comprehensive privacy policy for the application where Plaid Link will be deployed is essential. This policy should clearly outline how user data is collected, used, stored, and protected, as well as the rights of users regarding their personal information. Having a well-defined privacy policy not only helps ensure compliance with legal and regulatory requirements but also builds trust with users by demonstrating your commitment to protecting their privacy.

### Attest that your organization has implemented a data deletion and retention policy
**Description:** Implementing a defined and enforced data deletion and retention policy is crucial for ensuring compliance with applicable data privacy laws and for managing data securely and efficiently. It is recommended that your organization develop such a policy to outline the retention periods for various types of data and establish procedures for securely deleting data that is no longer needed. Regular reviews and updates of this policy are essential to stay aligned with changing legal requirements and to minimize the risks associated with data breaches and unnecessary data retention.
